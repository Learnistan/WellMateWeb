Mental Wellmate App — MVP Research

An empirical validation and literature mapping framework  ·  Published: July 2026

This document provides an empirical validation and literature mapping framework for the Mental Wellmate App. The purpose is to connect each visible app feature with established psychological constructs, research literature, and evidence-based mental health principles.

The app includes mood check-ins, body feeling check-ins, breathing exercises, body scan, hydration check, mindful movement, journaling, mindful games, reminders, personal journeys, progress tracking, language selection, login/profile, and contact/support features.

The app should be understood as a digital self-care and psychosocial support tool, not as a medical diagnostic tool or replacement for professional therapy. Mental health apps can support self-monitoring, coping skills, and wellbeing, but their effectiveness, privacy, cultural appropriateness, and safety must be evaluated carefully [11, BMJ Open, 2025], [12, Rickard et al., 2022], [13, Iwaya et al., 2022].

According to the World Health Organization, mental health is a state of wellbeing that helps people cope with life stress, realize their abilities, learn and work well, and contribute to their community [15, World Health Organization, 2025]. This makes the Mental Wellmate App relevant for women's mental health because many women experience stress, social pressure, family responsibilities, isolation, uncertainty, and limited access to professional mental health care.

2. Mood Check-In Feature

The app asks users: "How are you feeling today?" and provides mood or emoji-based options.

Validated Psychological Construct

This feature maps to affect labeling, emotional awareness, emotional self-monitoring, and emotion regulation.

Purpose

The purpose of the mood check-in is to help users identify their emotional state. Many people feel emotionally heavy but cannot clearly say whether they are sad, anxious, angry, tired, lonely, or hopeless.

Benefits

This activity helps users develop emotional awareness. It can help women notice patterns in their mood, such as feeling anxious before exams, sad after isolation, stressed after family conflict, or tired after poor sleep.

Expected Mental Health Effects

Mood check-ins may improve emotional awareness, early recognition of distress, and help-seeking behavior. Naming emotions is important because affect labeling has been shown to reduce emotional intensity and support implicit emotion regulation [1, Torre & Lieberman, 2018].

Literature Mapping

Torre and Lieberman explain that putting feelings into words can reduce emotional intensity and can work as an emotion regulation strategy [1, Torre & Lieberman, 2018]. Therefore, the mood check-in feature is theoretically supported as a low-intensity emotional regulation tool.

3. Mood Calibration Feature

The app asks deeper questions such as how pleasant the mood feels, how calm or energized the user feels, what the feeling may be, and what is contributing to the mood.

Validated Psychological Construct

This feature maps to emotional differentiation, emotional literacy, affect labeling, and cognitive-emotional awareness.

Purpose

The purpose is to move users from a general feeling to a more specific emotional understanding. For example, instead of saying "I feel bad," the user may realize, "I feel anxious because I am worried about my future."

Benefits

Mood calibration helps users understand emotional triggers. It can improve emotional vocabulary and make it easier for women to explain their feelings to a counselor, teacher, doctor, family member, or mental health practitioner.

Expected Mental Health Effects

This feature may reduce emotional confusion, increase self-understanding, and improve emotional communication. It may also help women identify whether their mood is connected to family pressure, social isolation, study stress, sleep problems, or future uncertainty.

Literature Mapping

Affect labeling research supports the idea that naming emotions contributes to emotional regulation [1, Torre & Lieberman, 2018]. Therefore, mood calibration can be viewed as a more detailed form of affect labeling.

4. Body Feeling Check-In Feature

The app asks users how their body feels, including options such as relaxed, tightness in the chest or shoulders, tiredness, or heaviness.

Validated Psychological Construct

This feature maps to somatic awareness, interoception, stress recognition, and mind-body connection.

Purpose

The purpose is to help users understand that mental stress can appear physically in the body.

Benefits

Many women may not say, "I am stressed," but they may experience headache, shoulder tension, chest tightness, tiredness, stomach discomfort, poor sleep, or body heaviness. This feature helps users recognize physical signals of emotional distress.

Expected Mental Health Effects

The body feeling check-in may improve early stress recognition, body awareness, and self-care behavior. It may help users choose appropriate coping strategies such as breathing, movement, rest, or journaling.

Literature Mapping

Kerekes et al. found links between psychological distress and somatic complaints among high school students, including physical symptoms connected to emotional strain [3, Kerekes et al., 2021]. Therefore, the body feeling check-in can be mapped to somatic stress awareness.

5. Interactive Body Scan Feature

The app shows a body image and allows users to identify areas where they feel tension, stress, or discomfort.

Validated Psychological Construct

This feature maps to body scan meditation, mindfulness, interoceptive awareness, and somatic stress mapping.

Purpose

The purpose is to help users locate stress in the body and understand the relationship between physical sensations and emotional experiences.

Benefits

The body scan helps users notice where they hold tension, such as the head, chest, shoulders, stomach, back, or legs. This is useful for women who experience emotional stress as physical pain or heaviness.

Expected Mental Health Effects

This feature may improve body awareness, reduce emotional confusion, and support early stress management. It may also encourage users to practice calming activities when they notice physical stress signs.

Literature Mapping

Fincham et al. reviewed body scan meditation and found that body scan practice is associated with mindfulness and body awareness, while also noting that the evidence should be interpreted carefully because body scan alone may not be enough for all mental health outcomes [4, Fincham et al., 2022].

6. Slow Breathing Activity

The app includes guided breathing with instructions such as inhale, hold, and relax.

Validated Psychological Construct

This feature maps to diaphragmatic breathing, relaxation response, physiological self-regulation, and stress reduction.

Purpose

The purpose is to help users calm their body and mind during stress, anxiety, anger, fear, panic, or emotional overwhelm.

Benefits

Slow breathing is simple, private, free, and easy to practice at home. It can be used before sleep, before exams, after family conflict, or during moments of fear and pressure.

Expected Mental Health Effects

This activity may reduce immediate stress, slow racing thoughts, improve calmness, and support emotional control. It may also help users regain focus during difficult moments.

Literature Mapping

Ma et al. found that diaphragmatic breathing improved sustained attention, reduced negative affect, and affected cortisol levels, which are connected to stress response [2, Ma et al., 2017]. A later meta-analysis also found that breathwork may reduce self-reported stress, anxiety, and depression symptoms, although more high-quality research is still needed [5, Fincham et al., 2023].

7. Hydration Check Activity

The app includes a hydration check where users can record drinking water.

Validated Psychological Construct

This feature maps to basic physical self-care, self-monitoring, and health behavior tracking.

Purpose

The purpose is to remind users that mental health is connected with physical care and basic body needs.

Benefits

Hydration check-ins encourage women to pay attention to their own body needs. Women often care for family members, children, students, or household responsibilities before caring for themselves.

Expected Mental Health Effects

Hydration tracking may support energy, attention, mood stability, and self-care habits. It also teaches that caring for the body is part of caring for the mind.

Literature Mapping

Masento et al. reviewed evidence showing that hydration status and water consumption can influence some cognitive abilities and mood states [6, Masento et al., 2014]. Therefore, hydration check-ins can be justified as a supportive self-care feature, not as a direct mental health treatment.

8. Mindful Movement Activity

The app includes mindful movement with tracking such as time, distance, calories, or duration.

Validated Psychological Construct

This feature maps to physical activity, behavioral activation, stress reduction, and mood improvement.

Purpose

The purpose is to encourage gentle movement as part of mental health self-care.

Benefits

Movement can improve energy, reduce body tension, support sleep, and improve mood. For women who cannot easily go outside because of safety, cultural, family, or social restrictions, indoor movement can be helpful.

Expected Mental Health Effects

Mindful movement may reduce stress, sadness, body heaviness, and anxiety. It may also improve motivation, body confidence, sleep quality, and daily routine.

Literature Mapping

The World Health Organization states that physical activity provides significant mental health benefits, reduces symptoms of depression and anxiety, enhances brain health, and improves overall wellbeing [7, World Health Organization, 2024].

9. Morning Intention Activity

The app includes morning prompts such as:

  • "Today I will focus on…"
  • "I will be mindful of…"
  • "I will care for myself by…"

Validated Psychological Construct

This feature maps to goal setting, self-efficacy, intentional behavior, and daily planning.

Purpose

The purpose is to help users begin the day with direction, self-care awareness, and a small achievable goal.

Benefits

Morning intentions can help women feel more organized and purposeful. This is important for women facing uncertainty, isolation, or limited control over their environment.

Expected Mental Health Effects

This activity may increase motivation, hope, self-efficacy, and emotional stability. It may reduce helplessness by helping users focus on small actions they can control.

Literature Mapping

WHO explains that mental health is shaped by individual, social, economic, and environmental factors [15, World Health Organization, 2025]. Small daily routines and supportive behaviors can contribute to mental health promotion. Therefore, morning intention-setting can be mapped to preventive mental health promotion and self-efficacy support.

10. Evening Reflection / Journaling Feature

The app includes evening reflection activities such as Three Wins Today and One Challenge, One Lesson.

Validated Psychological Construct

This feature maps to expressive writing, emotional processing, reflection, meaning-making, and self-awareness.

Purpose

The purpose is to help users reflect on the day, express emotions, identify positive moments, and learn from challenges.

Benefits

Journaling provides a private emotional space. This is especially useful for women who may not feel safe or comfortable sharing their feelings openly.

Expected Mental Health Effects

Journaling may reduce overthinking, improve emotional expression, support self-understanding, and help users process daily stress more calmly.

Literature Mapping

Pennebaker and Beall introduced expressive writing as a method for confronting emotional experiences [9, Pennebaker & Beall, 1986]. The American Psychological Association also describes expressive writing as a tool that can help people work through life challenges and support mental health [10, American Psychological Association, 2024].

11. Three Wins Today Activity

The app asks users to write or think about three positive things they achieved during the day.

Validated Psychological Construct

This feature maps to positive reflection, gratitude, self-recognition, self-efficacy, and strength-based coping.

Purpose

The purpose is to help users notice small successes and daily progress.

Benefits

This activity helps women recognize that even small achievements are meaningful. It can reduce feelings of uselessness, hopelessness, or failure.

Expected Mental Health Effects

Three Wins Today may increase self-confidence, gratitude, motivation, positive thinking, and hope.

Literature Mapping

This feature is connected to expressive writing and positive reflection. Expressive writing literature supports structured writing as a way to process experience and improve psychological wellbeing [9, Pennebaker & Beall, 1986], [10, American Psychological Association, 2024].

12. One Challenge, One Lesson Activity

The app asks users to identify one challenge they faced and one lesson learned from it.

Validated Psychological Construct

This feature maps to cognitive reframing, resilience, problem-solving, reflective coping, and meaning-making.

Purpose

The purpose is to help users understand difficult experiences in a constructive way.

Benefits

This activity teaches users that challenges can become sources of learning. It encourages emotional maturity and problem-solving instead of helplessness.

Expected Mental Health Effects

This feature may increase resilience, hope, coping ability, and emotional strength.

Literature Mapping

This activity can be mapped to cognitive reframing and resilience-building because it encourages the user to reinterpret difficulty as a source of learning. Reflective writing and meaning-making are supported in expressive writing literature, where writing is used to process emotional experiences and create understanding [9, Pennebaker & Beall, 1986], [10, American Psychological Association, 2024].

13. Mindful Games Feature

The app includes mindful games such as Visualizing, Positive Bubbles, and Emotions Sorting.

Validated Psychological Construct

This feature maps to attention training, emotional literacy, cognitive reframing, positive attention, and digital engagement.

Purpose

The purpose is to make mental health activities more interactive, enjoyable, and engaging.

Benefits

Mindful games may help users practice emotional skills without feeling that they are doing a difficult mental health exercise. Games can reduce boredom and increase app engagement.

Expected Mental Health Effects

Mindful games may improve focus, positive thinking, emotional recognition, relaxation, and continued app use.

Literature Mapping

Systematic reviews of smartphone mental health apps show that digital tools may be accessible and acceptable, but user engagement and long-term use are major challenges [11, BMJ Open, 2025]. Interactive features such as games may help improve engagement.

14. Visualizing Game

The app shows a word, such as "house," and asks the user to visualize it in the mind.

Validated Psychological Construct

This feature maps to focused attention, mental imagery, attention regulation, and mindfulness-based focus.

Purpose

The purpose is to help users focus on one mental image instead of overthinking.

Benefits

Visualization may help users redirect attention from negative thoughts to a neutral or calming mental image. It can be useful for students and women who struggle with concentration because of stress or anxiety.

Expected Mental Health Effects

This feature may reduce overthinking, improve concentration, and create calmness.

Literature Mapping

Focused attention and mental imagery are commonly studied within mindfulness and meditation research. Such practices are connected with attention regulation and mental training [14, Fox et al., 2016].

15. Positive Bubbles Game

The user taps positive words and avoids negative words.

Validated Psychological Construct

This feature maps to positive attention training, cognitive reframing, emotional awareness, and positive word recognition.

Purpose

The purpose is to train users to notice positive words and ideas.

Benefits

Women experiencing negative self-talk or hopelessness may benefit from repeatedly noticing positive words such as hope, strength, calmness, courage, and peace.

Expected Mental Health Effects

This activity may support positive thinking, emotional hopefulness, attention control, and reduction of negative focus.

Literature Mapping

This feature is connected to affect labeling and language-based emotional processing. Torre and Lieberman show that putting emotions and emotional words into language can influence emotional experience and regulation [1, Torre & Lieberman, 2018].

16. Emotions Sorting Game

The app includes a game where users identify or sort emotions.

Validated Psychological Construct

This feature maps to emotional literacy, emotion recognition, affect labeling, and emotional regulation.

Purpose

The purpose is to help users recognize and classify emotions.

Benefits

When users can name emotions, they can communicate feelings more clearly and choose healthier coping strategies.

Expected Mental Health Effects

Emotion sorting may improve emotional vocabulary, communication, self-awareness, and readiness to ask for help.

Literature Mapping

Affect labeling research supports the idea that naming emotions helps regulate emotional experience [1, Torre & Lieberman, 2018]. Therefore, emotion sorting can be justified as an emotional literacy activity.

17. Sleep Reminder

The app includes a sleep reminder.

Validated Psychological Construct

This feature maps to sleep hygiene, routine building, emotional regulation, and self-care behavior.

Purpose

The purpose is to encourage users to maintain a healthier sleep routine.

Benefits

Good sleep supports memory, concentration, emotional balance, patience, and energy.

Expected Mental Health Effects

Sleep reminders may help reduce emotional tiredness, irritability, poor focus, and stress. They may also support a more stable daily routine.

Literature Mapping

The CDC states that good sleep is essential for health and emotional wellbeing [8, Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, 2024]. Therefore, sleep reminders are relevant to mental health promotion and emotional stability.

18. Healthy Food Reminder

The app includes a reminder for healthy food or regular eating.

Validated Psychological Construct

This feature maps to physical self-care, routine support, health behavior prompting, and wellbeing maintenance.

Purpose

The purpose is to encourage users to eat regularly and care for their physical health.

Benefits

Eating regularly can support energy, focus, and physical strength. For women under stress, reminders may help prevent neglecting personal body needs.

Expected Mental Health Effects

Healthy food reminders may support energy, reduce weakness, improve concentration, and reduce irritability. This is not a direct treatment, but it supports overall self-care.

Literature Mapping

WHO explains that mental health is influenced by biological, social, and environmental factors [15, World Health Organization, 2025]. Basic physical self-care is part of creating conditions that support mental wellbeing.

19. Posture Reminder

The app includes a posture reminder.

Validated Psychological Construct

This feature maps to somatic awareness, physical self-regulation, body tension awareness, and stress-related posture monitoring.

Purpose

The purpose is to help users notice body position and reduce unnecessary physical tension.

Benefits

Good posture awareness may reduce neck, back, and shoulder discomfort. It can also remind users to pause and check their body during long phone use, study, or work.

Expected Mental Health Effects

Posture reminders may reduce body discomfort, improve body awareness, and support relaxation. Physical discomfort can increase stress and irritability.

Literature Mapping

Posture reminders can be mapped to somatic awareness because stress and emotional distress may appear through body tension, fatigue, and physical discomfort [3, Kerekes et al., 2021]. Therefore, posture reminders may help users notice how stress affects the body.

20. Personal Journey Feature

The app includes a journey section that organizes activities into a structured path.

Validated Psychological Construct

This feature maps to goal-directed coping, self-efficacy, structured behavior change, motivation, and resilience.

Purpose

The purpose is to guide users step by step instead of giving random activities.

Benefits

Journeys create structure, direction, and a feeling of progress. For women facing uncertainty or restrictions, this can support hope and motivation.

Expected Mental Health Effects

Personal journeys may improve consistency, motivation, hope, self-efficacy, and long-term engagement.

Literature Mapping

Digital mental health app research emphasizes the importance of feasibility, acceptability, and continued engagement [11, BMJ Open, 2025]. Structured journeys may support engagement by giving users a clear path and measurable progress.

21. Progress and Activity Tracking

The app tracks mood, activity completion, hydration, movement, and journey progress.

Validated Psychological Construct

This feature maps to self-monitoring, behavioral reinforcement, progress awareness, motivation maintenance, and self-efficacy.

Purpose

The purpose is to help users see their efforts over time.

Benefits

Progress tracking can help women recognize that small daily actions matter. It may encourage continued use of self-care activities.

Expected Mental Health Effects

This feature may increase motivation, confidence, hope, self-care consistency, and personal responsibility.

Literature Mapping

Mental health app reviews identify self-monitoring as one common function of digital mental health tools. Tracking features can support awareness and behavior change when designed safely and ethically [11, BMJ Open, 2025], [12, Rickard et al., 2022].

22. Language Selection Feature

The app includes language options such as English, Dari, and Pashto.

Validated Psychological Construct

This feature maps to cultural accessibility, linguistic inclusion, health communication equity, and user engagement.

Purpose

The purpose is to make mental health guidance understandable for local users.

Benefits

Dari and Pashto language options can make the app more accessible for Afghan women and girls who may not be comfortable using English.

Expected Mental Health Effects

Language access may increase comfort, trust, understanding, and continued use of the app.

Literature Mapping

UN Women reports that Afghan women and girls face major restrictions in education, employment, mobility, and public participation [16, UN Women, 2025]. UNICEF also reports that restrictions on girls' education and women's employment may reduce future access to female teachers and health workers in Afghanistan [17, UNICEF, 2026]. In such a context, local language mental health tools may reduce access barriers and support private learning.

23. Login and Personal Profile Feature

The app includes login and personal profile features.

Validated Psychological Construct

This feature maps to personalization, self-monitoring continuity, digital privacy, and data protection.

Purpose

The purpose is to create a personal space where users can save mood records, activities, reminders, journal entries, and progress.

Benefits

A profile can help users continue their self-care journey and return to previous records.

Expected Mental Health Effects

If privacy is strong, personal profiles may increase trust, continuity, and engagement. If privacy is weak, it may create fear and reduce app use, especially for women in unsafe or restrictive environments.

Literature Mapping

Iwaya et al. found that mental health apps create serious privacy concerns because they may collect sensitive personal data [13, Iwaya et al., 2022]. Therefore, login and profile features must include strong confidentiality, secure storage, and clear privacy information. See our Privacy Policy for how WellMate protects your information.

24. Contact or Support Feature

The app includes a contact or support section.

Validated Psychological Construct

This feature maps to help-seeking behavior, risk mitigation, user safety, and digital support.

Purpose

The purpose is to allow users to ask questions, report problems, or seek support.

Benefits

A support feature can increase trust and help users feel that assistance is available.

Expected Mental Health Effects

This feature may support help-seeking and reduce fear. The app clearly states whether the contact feature is for technical support, emotional support, or emergency help.

Literature Mapping

Rickard et al. emphasize the importance of quality, safety, and integrity in mental health apps [12, Rickard et al., 2022]. Support features should be designed carefully because users may depend on them during emotional distress.

25. Benefits of the Mental Wellmate App for Women

Emotional Benefits

The app may help women identify emotions, express feelings, reduce emotional confusion, and understand mood patterns. Mood check-ins, mood calibration, journaling, and emotion games are supported by affect labeling and expressive writing literature [1, Torre & Lieberman, 2018], [9, Pennebaker & Beall, 1986], [10, American Psychological Association, 2024].

Stress Management Benefits

Breathing, body scan, body feeling check-ins, journaling, and movement may support stress management. Breathing research shows improvements in attention, affect, and cortisol response [2, Ma et al., 2017], while body scan literature supports body awareness and mindfulness [4, Fincham et al., 2022].

Anxiety Management Benefits

Slow breathing, visualization, mood calibration, and journaling may help users manage worry, fear, and overthinking. Breathwork has been associated with reductions in stress and mental health symptoms in meta-analytic research [5, Fincham et al., 2023].

Self-Care Benefits

Hydration, sleep, food, posture, and movement reminders encourage women to care for their bodies and minds. Hydration can influence cognition and mood [6, Masento et al., 2014], physical activity reduces symptoms of depression and anxiety [7, World Health Organization, 2024], and good sleep is essential for emotional wellbeing [8, Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, 2024].

Confidence and Hope Benefits

Activities such as Three Wins Today, One Challenge, One Lesson, journeys, and progress tracking may support confidence, motivation, and resilience. These features are related to self-efficacy, positive reflection, reflective coping, and goal-directed engagement [9, Pennebaker & Beall, 1986], [10, American Psychological Association, 2024], [11, BMJ Open, 2025].

Educational Benefits

The app teaches users about emotions, body awareness, coping skills, breathing, journaling, self-care, and healthy habits. This supports mental health literacy and self-management [15, World Health Organization, 2025].

Benefits for Afghan Women

Because the app includes Dari and Pashto, it may be more suitable for Afghan women and girls. This is important because Afghan women and girls face restrictions affecting education, work, mobility, and public life [16, UN Women, 2025], [17, UNICEF, 2026].
